The Problem

Regional sales leads and operations managers at Vidyuttech were each maintaining their own Excel-based reporting trackers. By the time weekly numbers reached the executive layer, they had been manually consolidated across four teams — with no consistent definitions for key metrics like conversion rate, collection efficiency, or operational throughput.

The result was a recurring pattern: leadership would receive conflicting figures from different business units in the same meeting, and a significant portion of each review was spent reconciling numbers rather than acting on them.

"We'd spend the first 20 minutes of every Monday meeting figuring out which number was right. We needed one version of the truth."

Discovery & Data Modelling

Before touching Tableau, I spent the first week mapping the business logic behind every metric. Key questions I worked through with each stakeholder group:

  • What does "conversion" mean to sales vs. operations — are they measuring the same thing?
  • Which PostgreSQL tables are authoritative for each metric?
  • What's the desired granularity — daily, weekly, by region, by agent?
  • What decisions are actually being made from this data each week?

This discovery phase revealed three different definitions of "active customer" across teams. Aligning on a single SQL definition before building any views was the most valuable work I did on this project.

Build Approach

1
Unified Data Model

Wrote and documented a canonical SQL view layer on top of the raw PostgreSQL tables, enforcing agreed metric definitions. This became the single source of truth for all dashboards.

2
Dashboard Architecture

Designed a three-tier layout: an executive summary (top-line KPIs, trend lines), a regional drill-down (filterable by geography and time), and an agent-level detail view for operations leads.

3
Iterative Review Cycles

Ran two rounds of stakeholder review sessions — one with operations, one with leadership — before finalising. Feedback after round one led to adding a week-over-week comparison panel that became the most-used view.

4
Training & Handoff

Conducted a 45-minute walkthrough with each team lead and documented filter logic in a shared reference doc so the dashboards could be self-served without relying on me.

Key Challenges

  • Metric alignment — three different "conversion rate" definitions across teams. Resolution: ran a cross-functional alignment session and got written sign-off on unified definitions before building.
  • Slow query performance — initial Tableau extracts on raw tables were taking 8–12 minutes. Resolution: rewrote queries as materialised SQL views with appropriate indexes, bringing load time under 45 seconds.
  • Scope creep — each team wanted additional views added. Resolution: established a change-request process after the initial 15 dashboards shipped, keeping the core suite stable.

Results

  • 15+ dashboards deployed across sales, collections, and operations teams
  • 40% reduction in ad-hoc data requests as teams became self-sufficient
  • Monday review meetings shortened by approximately 30 minutes as metric conflicts eliminated
  • Executive summary dashboard became the primary reporting artefact used in leadership stand-ups

What I'd Do Differently

I'd formalise the metric alignment process earlier using a data dictionary document rather than relying on meeting notes. It would also have been worth setting up automated Tableau extract refreshes from day one — we did it manually for the first month, which introduced occasional data lag that eroded confidence until we fixed it.
